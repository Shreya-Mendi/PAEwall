RESEARCH · SLIDE 01 — PROBLEM & MOTIVATION  (0:30 – 1:07 · ~92 words)
================================================================

[Landed on Research page, Slide 01 section in view.]

"A patent attorney today reads claim language, guesses which
companies might infringe, hand-searches their 10-Ks, and builds
a claim chart line by line. Five hundred dollars an hour. Weeks
per patent. And most of the time she misses the right defendant
entirely.

The tools that fix this -- Patlytics, IP.com, ClaimChart LLM --
cost fifty thousand dollars a year. Individual inventors, small
PAE funds, university tech-transfer -- they are locked out.

Three gaps in the market: no public benchmark, no faithfulness
score on claim charts, no honest red-team view of enforcement."

================================================================

"Three models, one benchmark, four modules.

The benchmark is PAE-Bench -- five hundred and twenty-two

The pipeline: intake, retrieval, faithfulness-scored chart,

And the three models the rubric asks for: BM25 at thirteen

RESEARCH · SLIDE 03 — LIVE DEMO BRIEF  (1:44 – 2:21 · ~92 words)
================================================================

"Here's what you're about to see live. The demo patent is
US-2014-289857 -- a 2014 software patent for email-attachment
malware sandboxing. Open a suspicious attachment in a sandbox,
check for unexpected behavior.

In under ten seconds the system parses the independent claim
into six labeled sub-parts, retrieves the top candidate
infringers -- Microsoft, Palo Alto Networks, Symantec, Cisco,
Proofpoint -- highlights claim language against product text,
scores each limitation for faithfulness, and red-teams the case
with non-infringement and invalidity risk.

FastAPI, FAISS, Duke OIT LLM proxy. Sub-second retrieval."

[Scroll to Slide 04.]

RESEARCH · SLIDE 04 — RESULTS, INSIGHTS & FINDINGS  (2:21 – 3:00 · ~95 words)
================================================================

"Four takeaways.

One: dense retrieval wins. Plus one-fifty-two percent Recall-at-
ten over BM25, fifty-three percent in the software vertical.

Two: classical ML still wins MRR. Ranking is data-limited. Next
lever is a cross-encoder re-ranker.

Three: error analysis. Four of the top five BM25 failures are
IP-holding entities with empty 10-K product sections. That's a
data-coverage gap, not a model gap. Next semester: ITC and PTAB
expansion.

Four: the training curve plateaus at epoch three. The model is
hungry for data, not capacity. -- Ethics is built into the
product, not bolted on."

[Click "Home" in nav. Click "Start Analysis" → Protect mode card.]

"Microsoft at rank one, sixty-one percent enforcement

"Hover a row -- the matching part of the patent claim lights up.

verifier doing its job. Separate LLM call. Grounded in source
text. Not making things up."

[Click "Red Team" tab in workbench.]

"And this is the red team. Non-infringement arguments, one-oh-one
and one-oh-three invalidity risks, enforcement probability. The
honest answer, not the sales pitch."

LIVE DEMO · CHALLENGE MODE  (4:00 – 5:00 · ~155 words)
================================================================

[On Intent page. Click "Challenge Mode" card.]

"Same system, different intent. Challenge Mode -- I have been
accused of infringing, or I want to design around a patent.
Surface the weaknesses first."

[Enter US-2014289857-A1 again. Submit.]

"Same four-module pipeline. But now the ranking and the chart
re-weight toward invalidity signals and design-around surface."

[Workbench loads. Point at faithfulness split bar -- more
partial / not-found cells than in Protect.]

"Notice the faithfulness distribution shifts. In Challenge Mode
the system actively looks for limitations where the source text
is thin -- partial matches, not-found cells. Those are your
design-around openings."

[Click "Red Team" tab.]

"And the red team flips perspective. Instead of the patent
owner's enforcement case, it foregrounds prior art under
one-oh-two, obviousness combinations under one-oh-three,
one-oh-one subject-matter challenges.

Same pipeline. Same faithfulness verifier. Opposite intent. --
That is what makes PAEwall ethically defensible. Both sides get
the honest view."
