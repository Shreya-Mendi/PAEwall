================================================================
SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM  (50s · ~125 words)
================================================================

"Picture a patent attorney. She holds a patent worth maybe ten
million dollars -- but only if she can prove somebody is actually
infringing it.

So what does she do? She reads claim language. She guesses which
companies might infringe. She hand-searches product pages, 10-K
filings, marketing copy. She builds a claim chart -- line by line --
at five hundred dollars an hour. It takes weeks.  --> And most of
the time, she misses the right defendant entirely.

This is why the tools that fix this -- Patlytics, IP.com, ClaimChart
LLM -- cost fifty thousand dollars a year. Individual inventors,
small patent funds, university tech-transfer offices -- they're
locked out.

There's no public benchmark. No faithfulness score on the output.
No honest view of enforcement risk. -- That's the gap PAEwall fills."

[Advance to Slide 3.]

================================================================
SLIDE 3 — THE APPROACH  (50s · ~125 words)
================================================================

"Three ideas, one pipeline.

First -- I built my own benchmark: PAE-Bench. Five hundred and
twenty-two patent-defendant pairs pulled from real federal
litigation, across five technology verticals. It is the first
public cross-vertical patent-to-product retrieval benchmark of its
kind.

Second -- a four-module pipeline: intake, retrieval, faithfulness-
scored claim chart, and a red-team layer that co-generates the
defense's strongest counter-arguments. That last part is
deliberate: the tool cannot be used to push a weak case without
also surfacing why it's weak.

Third -- the deep learning. All three models the rubric asks for,
actually trained and shipped. BM25 gets thirteen percent
Recall-at-10. Classical TF-IDF jumps to twenty-four. My fine-tuned
dual encoder -- PatentSBERTa, InfoNCE contrastive loss, hard
negatives -- hits thirty-two point eight percent. -- That's a
one-hundred-fifty-two percent lift over keyword search."

[Pause. Breathe. Advance to Slide 4 — Live Demo.]

================================================================
SLIDE 4 — LIVE DEMO  (90s · stage directions + ~180 spoken words)
================================================================

"Let me show you this is real."

[Switch to browser tab: paewall-production.up.railway.app]
[Click 'Protect My Patent' card.]

"I'm going to use a real 2014 software patent -- US-2014-289857 --
for email-attachment malware sandboxing. Something we can all
intuit: open a suspicious attachment in a sandbox, check for
unexpected behavior, revert the machine if it's bad."

[Click US-2014289857-A1 button. Wait ~1 sec for results.]

"The top retrieved company is Microsoft. Rank one. Why? --"

[Scroll to show claim chart header; hover row 1[a].]

"This is the Patent Claim panel. Watch what happens when I hover
a row in Microsoft's claim chart --"

[Hover row 1[a] so the claim-text span lights up yellow highlighter.]

"-- the matching part of the patent claim highlights in real time.
That 1-bracket-a label ties Microsoft's Defender for Office 365
evidence directly back to the specific clause of the patent it
reads on."

[Point at faithfulness score on row 1[a]: 0.95]

"This row scored zero-point-nine-five because Microsoft's own
documentation explicitly describes Safe Attachments opening email
attachments in an isolated sandbox. That's the faithfulness
verifier doing its job -- a separate LLM call, grounded in source
text, not making things up."

[Scroll down to Red Team section for Microsoft.]

"And this is what makes it ethically defensible: the non-infringement
arguments, the invalidity risks, and an enforcement probability --
sixty-one percent for Microsoft. That is the honest answer, not the
sales pitch. If you wanted to build a weak troll case with this tool,
the tool would tell you it's weak."

[Switch back to slides. Advance to Slide 5.]

================================================================
SLIDE 5 — RESULTS & INSIGHTS  (50s · ~125 words)
================================================================

"Three takeaways.

One: dense retrieval is the right primitive. Plus one-fifty-two
percent Recall-at-ten. Software vertical alone gets to fifty-three
percent.

Two: classical ML still wins MRR. The dual encoder finds more
answers, but classical ranks them more precisely. Next lever is
a cross-encoder re-ranker -- I know exactly what to build.

Three: my error analysis found something I did not expect. Four of
the top five BM25 failures -- Valtrus Innovations, Clearly IP --
are IP-holding companies with empty 10-K product sections. That's
a data-coverage problem, not a model problem. It tells me the next
semester of work is benchmark expansion: ITC proceedings, PTAB
records. The training curve plateaus at epoch three. The model is
hungry for data.

Commercially: this is a vertical-SaaS wedge that's still empty.
Patlytics, IP.com -- none publish benchmarks. Four cents per query,
ten-dollar price point, ten-to-twenty-x gross margin. And the
ethics are built into the product, not bolted on."

[Advance to Thank You slide.]
