PAEwall — Automated patent infringement discovery.

"Given a patent, find who's infringing it — and prove it."

Three models · one benchmark · four-module pipeline. Below: the four slides that framed the demo, expanded with the full research record.

SLIDE 01

Problem & Motivation

A patent attorney today: reads claim language → guesses companies → hand-searches 10-Ks → builds a claim chart, line by line.

$500+/hour · weeks per patent · the right defendant is often missed entirely.

Tools that fix this — Patlytics, IP.com, ClaimChart LLM — cost $50k+/year. Individual inventors, small PAE funds, university tech-transfer, and in-house counsel are locked out.

The Problem, Formalized

Target selection is guesswork.

Patent monetization today relies on analyst intuition and keyword search over product disclosures. There is no public benchmark for "given claim text, predict which company infringes" — so retrieval models cannot be compared, and claim-chart generators cannot be evaluated for faithfulness. Early-stage case assessment is expensive, slow, and opaque to clients.

What's missing in the market

  • A public benchmark — no one publishes numbers.
  • A faithfulness score on LLM-generated claim charts.
  • A red-team module that gives both sides the same honest view of enforcement viability.

SLIDE 02

Approach Overview

Three models · one benchmark · four-module pipeline.

PAE-Bench — 522 patent–defendant pairs from real federal litigation (2015–2024), built from CourtListener + Google Patents BigQuery + SEC EDGAR 10-Ks. First public cross-vertical patent-to-product retrieval benchmark.

Patent → [ A. Intake ] → [ B. Retrieval ] → [ C. Faithfulness-Scored Chart ] → [ D. Red Team ]
Module A

Patent Intake

Parse independent claims from USPTO XML. Auto-extract into numbered sub-parts (1[a] … 1[f]). 483 patents × ~3.2 independent claims = 1,546 claim sets.

Module B

Retrieval

Score claims against 88 companies' 10-K Item 1 product descriptions. BM25 · TF-IDF + LogReg · dual-encoder fine-tuned on PAE-Bench.

Module C

Chart Synthesis

LLM produces a limitation → evidence map for top-k candidates. A faithfulness verifier scores each cell against source text to catch hallucination.

Module D

Red Team

Non-infringement arguments (§271) + invalidity (§101/102/103/112). A calibrated logreg head produces an enforcement probability.

Model Type Recall@10 Headline
BM25 Naive 13.0% Keyword search — claim language ≠ marketing copy
TF-IDF + LogReg Classical ML 24.1% Learned weights close part of the gap
Fine-tuned Dual Encoder Deep Learning 32.8% +152% over BM25, +36% over classical

Deep-learning recipe

PatentSBERTa (patent tower, 3.1M patent pre-train) · all-mpnet-base-v2 (product tower) · shared Linear(768→256) projection · InfoNCE contrastive loss with k=3 BM25-mined hard negatives · FAISS flat-L2 index for sub-second inference.

SLIDE 04

Results, Insights & Key Findings

Fine-tuned dense retrieval is the right primitive for patent → product matching.

+152% Recall@10 over BM25. The headline result, with an error analysis that points directly at what to do next.

Full retrieval results · 522 pairs, 5-fold CV

Retriever Recall@1 Recall@10 MRR Latency / query
BM25 (baseline) 0.1840.4210.27814 ms
TF-IDF + LogReg (ours · classical) 0.294 0.573 0.391 22 ms
Dual-encoder (ours · fine-tuned) 0.3680.6550.45241 ms
GPT-4o zero-shot 0.2410.4980.3292.8 s

Stratified by patent-family to prevent leakage.

INSIGHT 01

Software vertical wins biggest.

R@10 = 53.4% with the dual encoder — semantic match works when both sides are text-rich.

INSIGHT 02

Classical still wins MRR.

Ranking calibration is data-limited at n=49 test. Cross-encoder re-ranker is the next lever.

INSIGHT 03

Error analysis → coverage, not capacity.

4 of top-5 BM25 misses are IP-holding entities (Valtrus, Clearly IP) with empty 10-K product sections. Benchmark expansion (USITC §337 + PTAB IPR) is next semester's highest-leverage move.

INSIGHT 04

Training curve plateaus at epoch 3.

The model is hungry for data, not capacity. Confirms the "expand the benchmark" recommendation.

Faithfulness Protocol

Every claim-chart cell is scored against its source.

Supports

Source text fully maps to the limitation using terminology a court would recognize.

Partial

Source mentions the capability but omits a sub-element or substitutes a different mechanism.

Contradicts

Source explicitly disclaims or describes an incompatible implementation.

Not Found

Retrieval surfaced no relevant passage. Useful as a "design-around" signal.

Inter-annotator agreement (Cohen's κ) on a 120-cell gold set: 0.71 — substantial agreement.

Commercial Wedge

Unoccupied in legal-tech.

Public benchmark + faithfulness scoring + co-generated red-team output. Ethics is built into the product — every claim chart ships with a faithfulness score; every retrieval ships with the defense's strongest argument.

Ethics · Limitations

  • Retrieval benchmark, not a legal opinion. Ground truth reflects who was sued.
  • Skewed toward publicly-traded U.S. defendants; pre-IPO targets under-represented.
  • Enforcement probability is a prior, not a prediction.
  • Augments counsel, doesn't replace claim-construction judgment.

About

Built at Duke · MEng AIPI research.

PAEwall is an open research workbench combining neural retrieval, LLM-based claim chart synthesis, and faithfulness verification. The system uses BM25 + classical hybrid retrieval, trained on a 522-pair litigation corpus spanning 483 patents and 88 defendant companies.
